# Cover Letter

## Generic or Tailored?
For most tech jobs, not too much focus is placed on the cover letter, so it doesn’t need to be as polished as your resume. Most positions don’t require cover letters, but if there is a spot for it, you might as well include it. I wouldn’t direct it at a single company. Getting a job is a numbers game.  Anything that significantly slows down the process, like modifying your cover letter for each position, is not worth it. Only do this if you really care about a company you’re applying for. Keep your writing concise, and make sure that it fits on a single page.

## Introduction
Start by briefly stating why you are interested in a role along with a small professional introduction explaining who you are (name, major, college). Afterwards, describe what skills you have that would help in the position you’re applying to.

## Body
In your 2-3 body paragraphs, expand upon the skills you mentioned in your introduction paragraph. Provide a brief example which directly explains how you fulfill each skill from your introduction. Make sure that the focus remains on what you gained from each experience rather than the experiences themselves. Add details that they wouldn’t find from your resume. Then link the skills back to the job. For a generic cover letter, using skills that will help for most positions you apply to works fine.

## Conclusion
For your conclusion, just restate why you feel you are fit for this role. Keep this section as concise as possible to not retread too much ground. Thank the reader for their time to end off the document.
